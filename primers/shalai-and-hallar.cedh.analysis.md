# Shalai and Hallar — cEDH Primer Analysis

**Commander:** Shalai and Hallar {3}{R}{G} — 3/3 Legendary Elf Archer
**Color identity:** Gruul (RG)
**Source:** Moxfield cEDH primer by SteppinRazor (updated 2024-02-04)
**Theme:** Naya Combo-Stax; 1-card-plus-commander infinite damage combos via kicker triggers and +1/+1 counter placement; stax pieces slow the table while assembling fast creature-based wins

---

## Commander Ability (the trigger)

> Whenever you cast a spell, if mana from a kicked spell was spent to cast it, put a +1/+1 counter on each creature you control.
> Whenever one or more +1/+1 counters are put on Shalai and Hallar, it deals that much damage to each opponent.

Key constraints:
- **Two-part trigger chain** — the first ability fires when you cast a spell using mana generated by a kicked spell; the second fires whenever Shalai and Hallar receives counters from ANY source, not only the first ability
- **Kicker is a casting-condition dependency** — the first trigger requires that mana from a kicked spell was spent; no consumer card's oracle text says "kicker" — the producing cards say "kicked" in their own text; this link is invisible to oracle-text synergy detection
- **Damage scales with counters placed on Shalai and Hallar specifically** — distributing counters to all creatures is a bonus; what matters is how many land on the commander
- **Commander as finisher** — Shalai and Hallar is the win condition, not just an enabler; the deck assembles a loop that places counters on the commander repeatedly, dealing damage to all three opponents each iteration
- **Naya color identity ({R}{G}{W}) with only {R}{G} in casting cost** — white is available for stax pieces and protection creatures but not for the combo itself; the commander costs only {3}{R}{G}

## Primary Synergy Packages

### 1. Infinite counter-placement combos (1-card + commander wins)
The deck's primary gameplan is assembling one additional card alongside Shalai and Hallar to create an infinite loop:
- **The Red Terror**: with any red source dealing damage (combat or ability), The Red Terror triggers to put a +1/+1 counter on itself whenever a red source deals damage — this feeds Shalai and Hallar's second trigger; any red creature attacking sets off the loop
- **Heliod, Sun-Crowned**: pay {W}{1} to give Shalai and Hallar lifelink; attack with the commander → combat damage triggers Heliod → +1/+1 counter goes on Shalai and Hallar → Shalai and Hallar deals damage to each opponent → with lifelink that is life gain → Heliod triggers again → infinite damage loop
- **Emiel the Blessed + Dockside Extortionist**: Dockside ETB at a count of 4+; pay {G}{W} to flicker Dockside via Emiel → Emiel's secondary ability grants a +1/+1 counter on each flicker → Shalai and Hallar triggers → damage to all opponents; requires Dockside to produce at least 4 treasures (3 for Emiel activation + 1 for the secondary counter ability)

### 2. Creature tutors (assembling the one-card win condition)
The gameplan is entirely creature-based, making green's creature tutors the engine:
- **Finale of Devastation**: puts creature directly into play at X ≥ 10; also gives all creatures +X/+X as win accelerant
- **Chord of Calling**: instant-speed, convokable creature-to-battlefield tutor; finds combo pieces at end of opponent's turn
- **Eldritch Evolution**: sacrifice a creature to put a creature with CMC up to 2 greater into play; efficient combo assembler
- **Invasion of Ikoria**: searched creature goes directly to play; also a battle that can be flipped for upside
- **Eladamri's Call**: creature to hand with no restriction; pairs with Savage Summoning for uncounterable deployment
- **Worldly Tutor**: creature to top of library; cast before a draw step
- **Survival of the Fittest**: discard a creature, search for a creature; chains tutors to find exact pieces
- **Enlightened Tutor**: artifact/enchantment tutor that can find Heliod, Sun-Crowned (enchantment creature) — key cross-type tutor
- **Ranger-Captain of Eos**: tutors a 1-drop to hand (protection creatures) and can sacrifice itself as a Silence effect on the winning turn
- **Time of Need**: finds Kutzil, Malamet Exemplar (legendary silence creature) specifically

### 3. Protection suite (one-removal-spell protection)
cEDH combos require on-board protection since the win is interruptible by any removal spell:
- **Benevolent Bodyguard / Giver of Runes / Mother of Runes / Skrelv, Defector Mite**: tap to grant protection from a color; covers removal at instant speed; Skrelv also adds Toxic 1 (minor)
- **Cliffside Rescuer**: taps to give a creature protection from all colors until end of turn; broad protection for combo pieces
- **Shalai, Voice of Plenty**: gives all your creatures hexproof; blank prevents all targeted removal while in play; can also activate to put +1/+1 counters on all creatures (triggers Shalai and Hallar's second ability with mana)
- **Sylvan Safekeeper**: free: sacrifice a land to grant a creature shroud; zero-mana protection when no other option exists
- **Borimir, Warden of the Tower**: makes opponent's free spells cost {1}; provides stax AND can be sacrificed to remove its own restriction when you need opponents to be able to interact with another opponent's win attempt
- **Grand Abolisher / Ranger-Captain of Eos / Kutzil, Malamet Exemplar**: silence effects; shut down all opponent interaction on the winning turn

### 4. Stack interaction (Gruul-appropriate counterspell substitutes)
Gruul has no counterspells; the deck builds around this with uncounterability and speed:
- **Allosaurus Shepherd**: all your green spells cannot be countered; the backbone of anti-counterspell coverage
- **Veil of Summer**: all your spells this turn cannot be countered by blue or black; also draws a card; the most mana-efficient protection spell
- **Deflecting Swat**: redirect any spell or ability targeting you or a permanent you control; can redirect a counterspell targeting your combo piece back at the opponent's counterspell or critical permanent
- **Vexing Shusher**: pay {R}{G} to make any spell uncounterable; applies to opponent spells as well (important for letting opponents counter each other's win attempts)
- **Destiny Spinner**: creatures and enchantments cannot be countered; passive protection for the entire creature suite
- **Savage Summoning**: cast a creature spell at instant speed with a +1/+1 counter; the creature cannot be countered AND has a counter on ETB — this puts a counter on Shalai and Hallar immediately if both are in play, and the whole sequence is uncounterable
- **Red Elemental Blast / Pyroblast**: hard counters targeting blue spells only; the deck's only true counterspells
- **Legolas's Quick Reflexes**: split-second protection that also untaps a mana dork; essentially impossible to interact with

### 5. Stax pieces (slowing the table to match the deck's speed)
Selective stax that does not impair the creature-heavy, rock-light gameplan:
- **Drannith Magistrate**: stops commanders from being cast from the command zone; one of the most powerful hatebears in the format
- **Deafening Silence**: limits non-creature spell casting to one per turn per player; does not impair this deck's creature-based gameplan at all
- **Aven Mindcensor**: flash creature that replaces library searches with top-four looks; best tutor hate available in Naya
- **Linvala, Keeper of Silence**: shuts down activated abilities of opponent creatures; also a large beater for the beats plan
- **Blind Obedience**: artifacts and creatures enter tapped; stops Dockside combos and slows artifact ramp in a treasure-heavy meta
- **Soulless Jailer / Rest in Peace**: graveyard hate for reanimator strategies
- **Dauntless Dismantler**: artifact removal on a body; can destroy all artifacts costing {2} or less repeatedly to dismantle mana rock packages

### 6. Fast mana (speed matching the cEDH metagame)
- **Mana Crypt / Chrome Mox / Mox Diamond / Lotus Petal / Sol Ring**: the full cEDH fast mana package
- **Elvish Spirit Guide / Simian Spirit Guide**: exile for {G} or {R} respectively; zero-mana mana
- **Tinder Wall**: sacrifice for {R}{R}; fast red mana for a {G} creature
- **Rite of Flame**: sorcery for {R} that produces {R}{R} or {R}{R}{R} with multiples in the graveyard
- **Bloom Tender**: taps for {W}{G}{R} depending on board state; exceptional value in a Naya commander deck
- **Gaea's Cradle**: taps for {G} per creature; outstanding in a creature-dense deck
- **Delighted Halfling**: makes legendary spells uncounterable while producing one mana of any color

## Combo Lines

### Line 1: Shalai and Hallar + The Red Terror (any red damage source)
- Requirements: Shalai and Hallar on field, The Red Terror on field, any creature dealing red combat damage
- Loop: Any red source deals damage (including The Red Terror's own combat damage) → The Red Terror gets a +1/+1 counter → Shalai and Hallar's second trigger fires for each counter placed on it → Shalai and Hallar deals 1 damage to each opponent → repeat on each damage instance in combat
- Result: Infinite (or arbitrarily large) damage to each opponent simultaneously
- Win: All three opponents at 0 life simultaneously; cannot be survived by life gain (damage happens in a loop)

### Line 2: Shalai and Hallar + Heliod, Sun-Crowned (lifelink combat loop)
- Requirements: Shalai and Hallar on field, Heliod, Sun-Crowned on field, pay {W}{1} to grant Shalai and Hallar lifelink before combat
- Loop: Attack with Shalai and Hallar → deals combat damage → lifelink triggers life gain → Heliod's triggered ability puts a +1/+1 counter on Shalai and Hallar → Shalai and Hallar deals damage to each opponent equal to counters placed → that damage with lifelink gains more life → Heliod triggers again
- Result: Arbitrarily large damage to each opponent; requires combat so can only fire once per turn (without haste/untapping)
- Win: Sufficient damage in one combat step to kill all opponents; Shalai, Voice of Plenty pre-adding counters before combat can accelerate the counter count

### Line 3: Shalai and Hallar + Emiel the Blessed + Dockside Extortionist (Dockside count ≥ 4)
- Requirements: Shalai and Hallar on field, Emiel the Blessed on field, Dockside Extortionist in hand, opponents having enough artifacts/enchantments that Dockside ETB creates ≥ 4 treasures
- Loop: Cast Dockside Extortionist → creates 4+ treasures → pay {3} to activate Emiel's flicker ability on Dockside + pay {G}{W} for Emiel's secondary counter-grant ability → Dockside is exiled and returns → creates 4+ treasures again → Shalai and Hallar gets a +1/+1 counter from Emiel's secondary ability → deals 1 damage to each opponent → repeat indefinitely
- Result: Infinite ETBs, infinite treasures, infinite damage to all opponents
- Win: All opponents die to repeated damage triggers; treasures are also a mana surplus for any other response

### Line 4: Savage Summoning deploy (uncounterable combo piece + instant counter)
- Requirements: Shalai and Hallar on field, Savage Summoning in hand, final combo piece in hand (Heliod or The Red Terror)
- Sequence: Cast Savage Summoning → next creature spell this turn is uncounterable and gets a +1/+1 counter → cast Heliod or The Red Terror → it enters with a counter → if that counter placement triggers Shalai and Hallar, immediate damage before opponents can respond → transition to the infinite loop
- Result: Counter-proof combo assembly; even if opponents hold up interaction, the creature resolves and counter placement begins
- Win: As per Line 1 or Line 2 depending on which piece was deployed

## Deckbuilding Principles Stated by Author

1. **Protection over combo redundancy** — the combos are easy to assemble but a single removal spell stops them; run more protection pieces than extra combo lines.
2. **Stax that doesn't impair your own gameplan** — only run stax pieces that do not slow down a creature-heavy, creature-tutor-based strategy; Deafening Silence is safe; Collector Ouphe is not (it shuts off mana rocks you also run).
3. **Creature tutors over draw engines** — in Gruul with no card advantage in the command zone, creature tutors are the draw engine; the density of "put creature into play" effects compensates for no blue cantrips.
4. **Silence on the winning turn is mandatory** — Ranger-Captain of Eos, Grand Abolisher, and Kutzil must be available at the winning moment; without a silence effect, any instant-speed removal ends the loop.
5. **Gaea's Cradle is exceptional here** — the high creature density makes Cradle produce more mana than any rock; treat it as the primary ramp land.
6. **Fast mana push toward turn-3 wins** — adding the full fast mana package (Crypt, Mox Diamond, Chrome Mox, spirit guides) converted this from a midrange stax deck into a "catch them off guard" fast combo deck; the slower list could run out of time; the fast list wins before opponents set up.
7. **Beats plan is a fallback, not a strategy** — if infinite combos become impossible, large creatures can apply pressure; Linvala and Champion of Lambholt can threaten in combat, but this is not the primary plan.
8. **Vexing Shusher is bidirectional** — can protect your spells AND allow opponents to counter each other's win attempts; situational awareness required.

## Synergy Gaps vs. Current Pipeline

| Gap | Description | Example cards |
|---|---|---|
| Kicker-mana dependency is invisible to consumers | Shalai and Hallar's first trigger fires when mana from a kicked spell was spent to cast another spell; no consumer card's oracle text says "kicker" — the producing card (e.g., Dockside Extortionist cast with kicked-mana) carries the word "kicked" only in its own rules text, not in any synergy-edge oracle pattern that the pipeline can read from consumer side | Shalai and Hallar's trigger (no consumer says "kicker"; no edge exists) |
| Commander-as-finisher counter placement | +1/+1 counter placement from ANY source triggers Shalai and Hallar's second ability; the pipeline models counter-placement synergies as "producer puts counter on consumer" but cannot know that placing a counter on this specific commander also deals damage to all opponents | Heliod, Sun-Crowned; Shalai, Voice of Plenty; Savage Summoning — all trigger the damage via counter placement on the commander |
| Mana-generation-from-combat-damage loop | The Heliod combo requires tracking: attack → damage → lifelink → life gain → Heliod trigger → counter → damage → repeat; this is a multi-step transitive trigger chain across three different game zones/events; the pipeline's pairwise synergy edges cannot represent this three-way relationship | Shalai and Hallar + Heliod, Sun-Crowned + lifelink (three-card dependency) |
| Silence-effect creatures as combo protection | Grand Abolisher, Kutzil, and Ranger-Captain of Eos are most valuable on the turn you go for the win, not as ongoing stax; the pipeline cannot model the role of "silence on the critical turn" — it would see these as generic stax creatures with no connection to the combo | Grand Abolisher, Kutzil (Malamet Exemplar), Ranger-Captain of Eos — combo-protection role invisible |
| Uncounterable-delivery timing (Savage Summoning) | Savage Summoning's value is as a timing exploit: deploy the final combo piece at instant speed, uncounterably, with a free +1/+1 counter; the pipeline would see it as a weak instant but cannot model that it converts a sorcery-speed creature into a counterspell-proof threat | Savage Summoning (one-mana instant that deploys and protects a win in the same action) |
| Dockside count as meta-dependent threshold | The Emiel + Dockside combo requires that Dockside ETB creates at least 4 treasures (3 for Emiel flicker + 1 for counter grant); whether this threshold is met depends on opponents' artifact and enchantment count — a metagame variable the pipeline has no way to represent | Dockside Extortionist (value scales with opponent board state, not with any oracle text pairing) |
| Stax asymmetry (hurts opponents more than controller) | Deafening Silence limits non-creature spell casting; this is harmful to opponents but not to this deck because almost every win condition is a creature; the pipeline models Deafening Silence as a generic stax piece without knowing it is asymmetric in a creature-combo deck | Deafening Silence, Drannith Magistrate — correct stax picks only if the pipeline knows the deck is creature-only |
